Psychology For Software Professionals

For real-life software developers, psychology is the most important underlying science. Hard sciences such as electronics, predicate logic, and theory of computation do have specific applications. But mainstream software practice is all about psychology. The psychology of the user informs requirements analysis and user-interface design. The team dynamics and decision-making practices are covered by social psychology. And the tasks of design and programming are all about working within the cognitive capabilities of developers. Every professional practice is built on fundamentals

    Evolutionary Psychology

    Evolutionary psychology came to its mainstream fore in the late 1990s.
    Remember, "Men dominate the remote control because they're hunters
    surveying the landscape for prey. Women wear lipstick because ...?" If I
    had a dollar for every comedian who used quips like that, I'd be well on my
    way to $15 by now.

    The point scientists and historians have maed, though, is absolutely clear:
    we're still cavemen and cavewomen at heart. As with any popular theory, it
    may not be completely true, but there's lots to gain by considering its
    implications.
